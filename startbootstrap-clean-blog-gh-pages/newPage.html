Exploring Jerusalem

Experience the rich tapestry of history and culture in Jerusalem, the heart of the Holy Land. Walk the ancient cobblestone streets of the Old City, where sacred sites like the Western Wall, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, and the Al-Aqsa Mosque stand as testaments to the city’s diverse religious heritage. Explore vibrant markets such as the Mahane Yehuda, brimming with local produce, aromatic spices, and traditional delicacies. Venture beyond to the modern city, where world-class museums, lush parks, and cutting-edge cuisine await. Jerusalem is not just a city, but a journey through time, offering a unique blend of ancient allure and contemporary charm. Come, explore, and be captivated by the magic of Jerusalem.





Unique things to do:

  • Follow the footsteps of Jesus Christ during his last week. Go to the following sites in this order:
    1. Bethany
    2. Bethphage
    3. Pater Noster (Eleona)
    4. Dominus Flevit
    5. Orson Hyde Garden
    6. Cenacle
    7. Dormition Abbey
    8. Garden of Gethsemane
    9. St. Peter in Gallicantu
    10. Citadel of David and "The Hidden Gate" in the Builder's of Jerusalem Garden
    11. The Garden Tomb
  • See the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, Christianity's holiest site, in new ways:
    1. Wake up early to watch a priest open the church with the giant key
    2. Watch the spreading of the Holy Fire
      • This event happens once a year during Holy Week
      • Thousands of pilgrims unite in and around the Church of the Holy Sepulchre from all major congregations around the world
      • A priest enters the tomb of Christ where the flame is conjured
      • This flame is spread to all of the pilgrims by hand, candle to candle
      • In minutes, everyone is holding a lit candle
      • These pilgrims take these flames all around the world!
    3. Talk to the vendor Shaban to get access to the roof of the church and experience it from above
  • Visit the many restaraunts and shops that are found in the Old City. Many of them are especially fond of Latter-day Saints and the tourism they bring to the Holy Land
  • Go to the YMCA bell tower and play the carrillon bells over the Old City
  • Get a tour of the BYU Jerusalem center and enjoy the great views of the Old City from this historic building
